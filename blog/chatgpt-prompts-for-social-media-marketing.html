ChatGPT Prompts for Social Media Marketing

25 prompts we use every week for LinkedIn, X, and Instagram. Copy, paste, adapt. Each one tested on real brand accounts — not theoretical.

BR
The BuzzRiding TeamResearched and refined with AI tools

How to use these prompts

Each prompt below has a placeholder in brackets — swap it for your specific content, brand, or topic. The prompts are written to work with ChatGPT, Claude, or any comparable AI writing tool.

The best results come from giving the AI more context, not less. Before running any of these, prefix with: "You are writing for [brand name]. Brand voice: [describe it in 3 words]. Audience: [who they are]. Avoid: [anything off-brand]."

LinkedIn Prompts

Post from an article

Prompt 1 — Hook Post

"Turn this article into a LinkedIn post. Hook in the first line (no questions). 5 bullet takeaways. CTA to read the full article. Max 200 words. Article: [paste article]"

Prompt 2 — Insight Post

"Extract the single most counterintuitive insight from this article and expand it into a 150-word LinkedIn post. No external link. The post should stand alone. Article: [paste article]"

Prompt 3 — Data Post

"Write a LinkedIn post leading with this statistic: [statistic]. Explain what it means for marketers in 3 sentences. Add a practical takeaway. Max 120 words."

Original thought leadership

Prompt 4 — Opinion Post

"Write a LinkedIn post sharing an unpopular opinion about [topic in your industry]. Start with 'Unpopular opinion:'. Back it up with one specific reason. End with a question to drive comments. Max 100 words."

Prompt 5 — Lessons Learned Post

"Write a LinkedIn post structured as '5 things I learned from [experience/project]'. Each lesson in one sentence. Add a one-sentence reflection at the end. Max 180 words."

Prompt 6 — Trend Commentary

"Write a LinkedIn post reacting to this trend: [describe trend]. Take a clear position — for or against. Give one specific reason backed by evidence. Max 120 words."

X / Twitter Prompts

Prompt 7 — Single Insight Tweet

"Distil this article into one tweet under 240 characters. Lead with the most surprising data point or insight. No hashtags. No fluff. Article: [paste article]"

Prompt 8 — Thread Opener

"Write the opening tweet for a thread about [topic]. It should make someone stop scrolling. Under 180 characters. End with 'A thread:'"

Prompt 9 — Stat Tweet

"Write a tweet about this statistic: [statistic]. Make it feel surprising even if the reader has seen it before. Under 240 characters."

Prompt 10 — Contrarian Tweet

"Write a tweet that takes a contrarian position on [common belief in your industry]. Under 200 characters. No hedging."

Instagram Prompts

Prompt 11 — Caption from Article

"Write an Instagram caption for a post about [topic from article]. First line is the hook — no hashtags yet. 3-4 short paragraphs. End with a CTA. Add 5 relevant hashtags at the bottom."

Prompt 12 — Carousel Caption

"Write an Instagram caption for a carousel post covering [topic]. Tease what's inside without giving everything away. Under 150 words. Include a 'save this post' CTA."

Prompt 13 — Story Poll Hook

"Write 3 Instagram Story poll question options for a poll about [topic]. Each question should drive genuine curiosity. Format: Question + two answer options."

Repurposing Prompts

Prompt 14 — One Article → Full Week

"Take this article and create: (1) A LinkedIn hook post (200 words), (2) An X insight tweet (240 chars), (3) An Instagram caption (150 words + hashtags), (4) A newsletter teaser (100 words). Article: [paste article]"

Prompt 15 — Repurpose a Stat

"Turn this data point into 3 different social posts: one for LinkedIn, one for X, one for Instagram. Each should frame the stat differently for its platform audience. Stat: [paste stat]"

Prompt 16 — Turn a Quote into Content

"Take this quote and write one LinkedIn post and one tweet that use it as a launching point for a broader insight. Quote: [paste quote]"

Engagement & Community Prompts

Prompt 17 — Comment Reply

"Write 3 different replies to this LinkedIn comment. Each should acknowledge the commenter's point and add value. Tone: professional, warm. Comment: [paste comment]"

Prompt 18 — Community Question

"Write 5 open-ended questions I could post on LinkedIn to drive comments from marketing professionals. Topic: [topic]. Avoid yes/no questions."

Campaign & Launch Prompts

Prompt 19 — Product Launch Sequence

"Write a 5-post LinkedIn launch sequence for [product/service/report]. Post 1: tease. Post 2: problem. Post 3: solution reveal. Post 4: social proof. Post 5: CTA. Each post max 150 words."

Prompt 20 — Event Promotion

"Write 3 social posts promoting [event name] on [date]. Post 1 (2 weeks out): awareness. Post 2 (1 week out): urgency. Post 3 (day before): final reminder. Each under 120 words."

Content Ideation Prompts

Prompt 21 — Weekly Content Ideas

"Generate 10 social media post ideas for a [type of brand] targeting [audience]. Mix formats: data, opinion, tutorial, story, question. List format."

Prompt 22 — Trend Response

"[Industry trend or news]. Write 3 social post ideas that respond to this trend from the angle of [your brand's positioning]."

Prompt 23 — Monthly Theme

"Create a content theme for [month] for a [type of brand]. Suggest a theme, 4 content pillars for the month, and 2 post ideas for each pillar."

Analytics & Improvement Prompts

Prompt 24 — Post Audit

"Here are my 5 best-performing LinkedIn posts from last month: [paste posts]. Identify what they have in common, and write 3 new posts following those patterns for [new topic]."

Prompt 25 — Rewrite Underperformer

"This post got poor engagement: [paste post]. Rewrite it with: a stronger hook, a clearer insight, and a more direct CTA. Keep the same core message."

Frequently Asked Questions

Can ChatGPT write all my social media posts?
It can draft all of them. Whether you publish without editing is up to your quality standard. The best results come from using AI for first drafts and structure, then adding one specific, human detail that makes the post feel real. Fully unedited AI posts tend to feel generic over time.
What's the best ChatGPT prompt for LinkedIn?
The prompts that consistently produce the best LinkedIn output include: a clear voice instruction ("friendly, practical, data-led"), a specific audience description, and a word count limit. Prompt 1 and Prompt 14 in this list are the highest-performing starting points in our testing.
How do I make AI social posts sound less robotic?
Three things work reliably: give the AI a specific brand voice to match, add a real statistic or data point as an anchor, and after generating, manually rewrite the opening line in your own words. That combination consistently produces natural-sounding output.
