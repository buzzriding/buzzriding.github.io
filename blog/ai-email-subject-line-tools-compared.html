AI Email Subject Line Tools Compared

We ran 200 subject lines through five AI tools and measured real open rates over 30 days. One tool produced consistently better results — and it's not the one most marketers are using.

BR
The BuzzRiding Team Researched and refined with AI tools

Why subject lines are the highest-ROI AI use case in email

Your subject line determines whether everything else you wrote gets read. A 10% open rate improvement on a 5,000-person list is 500 additional readers per send — without changing a word of the email itself.

AI tools for subject line generation have proliferated. Most of them produce the same mediocre output dressed up in different interfaces. To find out which ones actually move the needle, we ran a structured 30-day test.

🔬 Test Methodology

200 subject lines generated across 5 tools, 40 per tool. Each tool received the same email brief. Subject lines were A/B tested on a 5,000-subscriber list across 10 email campaigns. Open rate was the primary metric. Click rate was tracked as a secondary signal.

The results — ranked by average open rate lift

1. Claude (with structured prompt) 🏆 Winner
+14.3%
Average open rate lift vs. human-written baseline

Claude produced the strongest subject lines — but only when given a structured prompt that included the email's purpose, target audience, and tone. Without context, Claude's output was average. With a good prompt, it consistently generated subject lines that outperformed our human-written baseline. The key differentiator: Claude's output was more specific and less reliant on tired formulas.

2. Jasper AI (Email Subject Lines template) Strong
+9.1%
Average open rate lift vs. baseline

Jasper's dedicated email subject line template produced consistently solid results. The output was more formulaic than Claude's — heavy on curiosity gaps and numbered lists — but the formulas work. Best suited for high-volume sends where consistency matters more than creativity.

3. ChatGPT (GPT-4) Good
+7.8%
Average open rate lift vs. baseline

ChatGPT performed well with a detailed prompt. The output quality is close to Claude's for short-form tasks. The gap appeared in specificity — Claude produced more brand-contextual lines, while ChatGPT defaulted to generic email marketing patterns more quickly without heavy prompting.

4. Phrasee Decent
+5.2%
Average open rate lift vs. baseline

Phrasee is a specialist email optimisation tool with its own language model. The output was safe and brand-consistent, but never surprising. It performed well on retention emails to engaged lists — less well on cold or re-engagement campaigns where you need something unexpected to break through.

5. Built-in ESP AI (generic) Skip
+1.4%
Average open rate lift vs. baseline

The AI subject line tools built into most email platforms (Mailchimp, HubSpot, Klaviyo) produced the weakest results. They're trained on broad datasets and have no context about your brand or audience. The lift was marginal and not worth relying on as a primary tool.

"The tool matters less than the prompt. Claude wins because it can hold more context — but a vague Claude prompt loses to a specific Jasper template every time."

The prompt that produced the best Claude subject lines

The top-performing subject lines from Claude came from one specific prompt structure. Here it is, ready to copy:

Claude subject line prompt
Email brief: [describe the email's main offer or topic in 1-2 sentences]. Target reader: [describe your audience — role, seniority, pain point]. Tone: [e.g. direct, curious, warm, urgent]. Avoid: clickbait, all-caps, excessive punctuation, generic phrases like "you won't believe". Generate 10 subject line options. For each one, note the psychological trigger it uses (curiosity, specificity, self-interest, urgency, social proof). Mark your top 3.

What makes a subject line actually work

Across 200 tested subject lines, the patterns that consistently drove higher open rates were:

  • Specificity over vagueness. "3 AI tools that cut our brief time by 40%" outperformed "How AI can save you time" by an average of 18% in our tests.
  • Reader self-interest in the first 4 words. Subject lines that front-loaded the benefit — before the hook — performed better on mobile where preview text is limited.
  • Mild pattern interruption. Unusual sentence structure or an unexpected word in an otherwise normal subject line outperformed pure curiosity gaps.
  • No emoji in B2B sends. Emoji in subject lines reduced open rates by an average of 6% on our professional marketing audience. On consumer lists this may differ.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best AI tool for email subject lines?
Based on our 30-day test across 200 subject lines, Claude produced the highest average open rate lift at +14.3% versus our human-written baseline — with a structured prompt. It's also free, which makes it the clear choice for most marketers. Jasper AI is the runner-up for teams that need volume at consistency and don't mind the monthly cost.
Do AI-generated subject lines actually improve open rates?
Yes — when used correctly. Every tool in our test outperformed our human-written baseline to some degree. The key is providing the AI with context: the email's purpose, your audience, the tone, and what to avoid. A contextless AI prompt produces generic output that won't move your numbers.
How many subject line options should I generate per email?
Generate 8–10, then shortlist to 2–3 for A/B testing. More than 3 variants creates statistical significance problems in most list sizes. The goal is to test meaningfully different approaches — curiosity vs. specificity, question vs. statement — not slight wording variations.
Can I use ChatGPT instead of Claude for subject lines?
Yes. ChatGPT with a detailed prompt produced a +7.8% open rate lift in our test — meaningful improvement over no AI assistance. The gap between Claude and ChatGPT narrowed significantly when both were given the same structured prompt. If you're already using ChatGPT, use it. If you're starting fresh, Claude's free tier is slightly stronger for this task.
Should I use the built-in AI tool in my email platform?
As a primary tool, no. Built-in ESP AI tools produced the weakest results in our test (+1.4% lift) because they have no context about your brand or audience. Use Claude or ChatGPT to generate options, then paste the best ones into your platform. The 2 minutes this takes is worth the additional open rate lift.
